# QuickDrop Terms

Last updated: July 15, 2026

QuickDrop is an early beta productivity tool for saving and organizing links, snippets, notes, images, PDFs, and document URLs.

## Acceptable Use

Do not use QuickDrop to store or share illegal content, harmful content, malware, private data you do not have permission to store, or content that violates another service's rules.

## User Content

Users are responsible for the content they save in QuickDrop. QuickDrop stores user-created metadata in Firebase Firestore and uploaded file URLs from the configured file provider.

## Beta Status

QuickDrop is still evolving. Features such as notifications, file deletion from the storage provider, AI summaries, and advanced organization may change before a full public release.

## Availability

QuickDrop may be unavailable during maintenance, Firebase or Cloudinary outages, development changes, or beta testing.

## No Warranty

QuickDrop is provided as-is during beta. Use it with reasonable backups for important information.

## Contact

For support, feedback, or account/data questions, use the contact address shown in the app or project page.
